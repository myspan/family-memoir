# Freeform Narration Mode v0.1

## Core stance

Do not over-constrain the speaker.

The first version should not force elders into a structured interview. It should let them begin wherever they want, jump around naturally, repeat, pause, and tell the story in the way that feels human to them.

## Product language

Preferred framing:

- "Start anywhere you like"
- "You don't need to go in order"
- "Talk about a person, place, time, or memory"
- "We'll help organize it afterward"

Avoid framing like:

- "Answer the following questions"
- "Choose the correct topic"
- "Please tell your life in chronological order"

## System behavior principles

1. Respect natural flow
2. Do not treat repetition or jumps as errors
3. Identify what the speaker cares about most
4. Preserve texture, not just facts
5. Only ask lightweight follow-ups when truly useful

## Output philosophy

The first result does not have to be a complete chapter.

A good fragment is better than a fake-complete chapter.

The output can be:

- a memory fragment
- a person-centered recollection
- a place-centered recollection
- a period-of-life fragment

## Follow-up philosophy

Follow-up questions should feel like natural continuation, not a survey.

Good:
- "You mentioned your mother was always busy — what's one memory that captures her best?"
- "You said moving changed everything — what changed the most?"

Bad:
- "Please describe your mother in detail."
- "What happened next chronologically?"
