# Product Draft v0.1

## One-line definition

An online memoir listener for family testing: upload an iPhone Voice Memo, let an elder speak freely, and get back a structured memory fragment draft plus a few natural follow-up questions.

## Target user

Initial user is the maker's own family.

This means the product should optimize for:

- low friction
- familiar input habits
- emotional safety
- strong quality of listening / writing
- simple output that feels worth saving

## Non-goals for v0.1

- realtime voice conversation
- rigid interview flow
- fancy editing suite
- multi-user collaboration
- full-book generation
- monetization design

## User flow

1. Family member records a voice memo on iPhone
2. They upload the `.m4a` file
3. System transcribes and understands the story
4. System returns:
   - a title
   - a memory fragment / chapter draft
   - selected quotes
   - focus summary
   - entities and unresolved details
   - 3-5 follow-up questions
5. Family optionally records a second memo answering one of the follow-up prompts

## Product promise

The system should feel less like a bot asking questions and more like a careful person who truly listened, then helped preserve what mattered.

## Success criteria

- The family member can upload without confusion
- The resulting draft feels like the elder, not generic AI prose
- Important people / scenes / emotional signals are preserved
- Follow-up prompts are genuinely useful
- The output is good enough to keep
